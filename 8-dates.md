<!-- ============ DATE 1 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-1">
  <span class="date-number">Date One</span>
  <h2>Lean on Me</h2>
  <p class="theme">Trust &amp; Commitment</p>

  <p>Trust is built in small moments of "turning toward" your partner. Commitment is the
  decision to cherish what you have rather than compare it to what you don't.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Reflect individually</strong> on what trust and commitment mean to you before the date.</li>
    <li>Share a <strong>story of a time your partner was there for you</strong> — and one time you wish they had been.</li>
    <li>Discuss the <strong>trust questions</strong>: When do you feel most trusted by me? What makes you feel safe in this relationship? When have I let you down, and how can I repair it?</li>
    <li>Discuss the <strong>commitment questions</strong>: What does commitment mean to you? What do you cherish about us? What tempts you away from us?</li>
    <li><strong>Create a "we" statement</strong> — something you both commit to moving forward.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 2 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-2">
  <span class="date-number">Date Two</span>
  <h2>Agree to Disagree</h2>
  <p class="theme">Addressing Conflict</p>

  <p>69% of all relationship conflicts are <em>perpetual</em> — they never fully resolve.
  The goal isn't to win, it's to understand the dreams inside each other's positions.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Identify one <strong>perpetual conflict</strong> you return to, and one <strong>solvable problem</strong> that's been annoying you.</li>
    <li>Share your <strong>conflict history</strong>: How did your family handle disagreement growing up? What did anger, repair, and silence look like?</li>
    <li>Practice the <strong>Speaker–Listener exercise</strong> — one person speaks from the "I", the other reflects back before responding.</li>
    <li>Uncover the <strong>dream within the conflict</strong>: behind your position, what are you really longing for, afraid of, or honoring?</li>
    <li>Offer each other a <strong>repair attempt</strong> and agree on a phrase to use mid-fight when things get heated.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 3 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-3">
  <span class="date-number">Date Three</span>
  <h2>Let's Get It On</h2>
  <p class="theme">Sex &amp; Intimacy</p>

  <p>Most couples have never actually talked about sex — they've only had it. Great lovers
  are <em>made</em> through conversation, not discovered.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Separately <strong>write down what great sex means to you</strong> — emotionally, physically, spiritually — then trade and discuss.</li>
    <li>Share <strong>your sexual history with each other</strong>: favorite memories together, moments of closeness, times you felt distant.</li>
    <li>Talk about <strong>how you like to be approached</strong>, what builds desire for you, and what kills it.</li>
    <li>Create a <strong>shared "love map" of intimacy</strong>: fantasies you're curious about, things you want to try, things that are off the table.</li>
    <li>Agree on a <strong>ritual of connection</strong> — a cue, a gesture, a habit that invites intimacy without pressure.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 4 — FEATURED ============ -->
<div class="date-card featured" id="date-4">
  <span class="date-number">Date Four · Featured</span>
  <h2>The Things We Can (and Can't) Buy with Money</h2>
  <p class="theme">Work &amp; Money</p>

  <p>Money fights aren't really about money — they're about what money <em>means</em>:
  security, freedom, power, love, status, worth. The goal of this date is to uncover each
  partner's "money story" and build a shared philosophy around work and resources before
  talking numbers.</p>

  <h3>Before the Date — Solo Reflection</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Write your <strong>Money Autobiography</strong>. Start with your earliest memory of money. How did your parents handle it? Was there enough? Was it talked about openly, or kept secret? Who made the financial decisions? What did money <em>mean</em> in your household — safety, status, stress, freedom?</li>
    <li>Identify your <strong>money symbols</strong>. Which of these does money most represent to you: <em>security, freedom, power, control, love, status, pleasure, independence, legacy</em>? Pick your top 2–3 and why.</li>
    <li>Name your <strong>financial fears and financial dreams</strong>. What's the worst-case scenario you quietly worry about? What's the life you'd build if money weren't in the way?</li>
    <li>Reflect on your <strong>relationship to work</strong>. Is your current work a job, a career, or a calling? What would you do if you didn't have to earn?</li>
  </ol>

  <h3>On the Date — Questions to Ask Each Other</h3>
  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 1 · Money Stories</p>
    <h4>Share your money autobiographies</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What is your earliest memory of money?</li>
      <li>How did your family talk about money — or avoid talking about it?</li>
      <li>Did you grow up feeling rich, poor, or somewhere in between? How did that shape you?</li>
      <li>What did your parents teach you about money, directly or by example?</li>
      <li>What money habits from your family do you want to keep? Which do you want to leave behind?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 2 · What Money Means</p>
    <h4>Compare your money symbols</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What does money symbolize for you today — security, freedom, power, love, status?</li>
      <li>When do you feel most anxious about money? Most generous? Most indulgent?</li>
      <li>Are you more of a spender or a saver — and where do you think that came from?</li>
      <li>What's a purchase you've made that felt <em>worth it</em>? What's one you regret?</li>
      <li>If we had $1,000 of truly unplanned money this week, what would each of us want to do with it?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 3 · Work &amp; Meaning</p>
    <h4>Talk about the meaning of your work</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What does your work mean to you — is it a job, a career, or a calling?</li>
      <li>What do you love about what you do? What drains you?</li>
      <li>If money weren't a factor, what would you be doing in five years?</li>
      <li>How does my work affect our relationship — for better or worse?</li>
      <li>What do you need from me to support your work and your dreams?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 4 · Fears &amp; Dreams</p>
    <h4>Share your financial fears and dreams</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What's your biggest fear about money — for us, for our future?</li>
      <li>What would "enough" look like for you?</li>
      <li>What's a dream of yours that money could either enable or block?</li>
      <li>If we had to drastically cut back, what would you <em>not</em> want to give up?</li>
      <li>What does a generous life look like to us — what do we want to give, and to whom?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 5 · A Shared Philosophy</p>
    <h4>Build the beginning of a shared money plan</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What are our <strong>top three shared financial goals</strong> for the next 1, 5, and 10 years?</li>
      <li>What's <em>our</em> philosophy on saving, spending, giving, and debt?</li>
      <li>How do we want to divide financial decisions — who handles what, and what do we decide together?</li>
      <li>What's a ritual or rhythm we can build (monthly money check-in, annual review) to keep this conversation going?</li>
      <li>Write one sentence together: <em>"In our relationship, money is for ______."</em></li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <p class="note">The Gottmans emphasize that you don't have to agree on everything — you have
  to <em>understand</em> each other. The numbers come later; the meaning comes first.</p>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 5 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-5">
  <span class="date-number">Date Five</span>
  <h2>Room for Two (or More)</h2>
  <p class="theme">Family</p>

  <p>Whether or not you want children, family is the deepest test of shared meaning:
  whose rituals win, whose wounds get repeated, whose values get passed on.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Share your <strong>vision for family</strong>: kids or no kids, how many, when, and why.</li>
    <li>Talk about your <strong>family of origin</strong>: what you want to carry forward, what you want to break.</li>
    <li>Discuss <strong>division of labor</strong> — paid work, house work, emotional work — and what feels fair.</li>
    <li>Name the <strong>values</strong> you most want to model and pass on (or live by, if kids aren't part of the plan).</li>
    <li>Talk about <strong>how you'll protect the relationship</strong> if/when kids, aging parents, or other family arrive.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 6 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-6">
  <span class="date-number">Date Six</span>
  <h2>Lifelong Love Needs a Playful Heart</h2>
  <p class="theme">Play &amp; Adventure</p>

  <p>Play is the antidote to contempt. Couples who keep adventuring together — in big ways
  and small — build resilience against routine.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Each of you lists <strong>five things you find genuinely fun</strong> — then look for overlap.</li>
    <li>Share a <strong>favorite memory of play</strong> from childhood, and one from your relationship.</li>
    <li>Build a <strong>shared bucket list</strong> of adventures: small (a day trip), medium (a weekend), big (a lifetime).</li>
    <li>Pick <strong>one adventure</strong> from the list and plan it together — actual dates, actual logistics.</li>
    <li>Agree on a <strong>weekly ritual of play</strong> — something low-stakes and recurring that keeps levity in the relationship.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 7 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-7">
  <span class="date-number">Date Seven</span>
  <h2>You, Me, and Us</h2>
  <p class="theme">Growth &amp; Spirituality</p>

  <p>A great relationship supports each person becoming <em>more</em> themselves. This date
  is about the inner life — what gives meaning, what you believe, how you're changing.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Share your <strong>spiritual or philosophical background</strong> — what you were raised with, what you kept, what you let go.</li>
    <li>Talk about <strong>what gives your life meaning</strong> now, and where you find awe.</li>
    <li>Name a way <strong>you've grown</strong> in the past few years, and a way you still want to grow.</li>
    <li>Discuss how you can <strong>support each other's growth</strong> — including the parts that are uncomfortable.</li>
    <li>Identify one <strong>shared practice</strong> (meditation, nature, service, ritual) that could belong to both of you.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<!-- ============ DATE 8 ============ -->
<div class="date-card" id="date-8">
  <span class="date-number">Date Eight</span>
  <h2>A Lifetime of Love</h2>
  <p class="theme">Dreams</p>

  <p>The final date is about legacy: the story you want to tell, together, at the end of
  your life. Honoring each other's dreams is the deepest act of love.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Each of you writes down <strong>three lifetime dreams</strong> — things you want to do, be, experience, or leave behind.</li>
    <li>Take turns being the <strong>dream-teller</strong> and the <strong>dream-catcher</strong>: one shares, the other listens without fixing.</li>
    <li>Ask: <strong>What does this dream mean to you?</strong> Where does it come from? What would it cost to let it go?</li>
    <li>Identify <strong>one dream you can honor together</strong> in the next year — and one small step toward it this month.</li>
    <li>Write a <strong>shared mission statement</strong>: if our relationship had a purpose, it would be ______.</li>
  </ol>
</div>
