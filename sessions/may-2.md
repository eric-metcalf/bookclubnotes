<div class="section-block">
  <h2>3 · Discussion Questions 💬</h2>
  <p class="section-time">25 minutes</p>
  <p>Pick the ones that feel interesting — no need to go in order.</p>
  <ol>
    <li>What's a small, recurring stressor in your daily life — traffic, email, a coworker, a household task — that you'd love to handle differently?</li>
    <li>Burns argues that it's not the event that stresses us, it's what we tell ourselves <em>about</em> the event. Can you think of a recent moment where two people had wildly different reactions to the same situation?</li>
    <li>Which cognitive distortion shows up most for you under everyday stress — should statements, mind reading, catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking? Pick one and give a real example.</li>
    <li>Burns suggests catching automatic thoughts in real time and writing them down. Have you ever tried this in the moment? What got in the way?</li>
    <li>Where in your week is stress actually a useful <em>signal</em> that something needs to change — versus stress that's just a story you could tell differently?</li>
    <li>What's a coping habit that's worked for you (a walk, a phrase, a friend, a deep breath) and what's one that you reach for that doesn't actually help?</li>
    <li>If you could only carry one tool from this chapter into next week, which one would you pick?</li>
  </ol>
</div>
