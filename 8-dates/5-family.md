  <span class="date-number">Date Five</span>
  <h2>Room for Two (or More)</h2>
  <p class="theme">Family</p>

  <p>Whether or not you want children, family is the deepest test of shared meaning:
  whose rituals win, whose wounds get repeated, whose values get passed on.</p>

  <h3>Exercises</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Share your <strong>vision for family</strong>: kids or no kids, how many, when, and why.</li>
    <li>Talk about your <strong>family of origin</strong>: what you want to carry forward, what you want to break.</li>
    <li>Discuss <strong>division of labor</strong> — paid work, house work, emotional work — and what feels fair.</li>
    <li>Name the <strong>values</strong> you most want to model and pass on (or live by, if kids aren't part of the plan).</li>
    <li>Talk about <strong>how you'll protect the relationship</strong> if/when kids, aging parents, or other family arrive.</li>
  </ol>
</div>
