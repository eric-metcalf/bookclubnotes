  <span class="date-number">Date Four · Featured</span>
  <h2>The Things We Can (and Can't) Buy with Money</h2>
  <p class="theme">Work &amp; Money</p>

  <p>Money fights aren't really about money — they're about what money <em>means</em>:
  security, freedom, power, love, status, worth. The goal of this date is to uncover each
  partner's "money story" and build a shared philosophy around work and resources before
  talking numbers.</p>

  <h3>Before the Date — Solo Reflection</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Write your <strong>Money Autobiography</strong>. Start with your earliest memory of money. How did your parents handle it? Was there enough? Was it talked about openly, or kept secret? Who made the financial decisions? What did money <em>mean</em> in your household — safety, status, stress, freedom?</li>
    <li>Identify your <strong>money symbols</strong>. Which of these does money most represent to you: <em>security, freedom, power, control, love, status, pleasure, independence, legacy</em>? Pick your top 2–3 and why.</li>
    <li>Name your <strong>financial fears and financial dreams</strong>. What's the worst-case scenario you quietly worry about? What's the life you'd build if money weren't in the way?</li>
    <li>Reflect on your <strong>relationship to work</strong>. Is your current work a job, a career, or a calling? What would you do if you didn't have to earn?</li>
  </ol>

  <h3>On the Date — Questions to Ask Each Other</h3>
  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 1 · Money Stories</p>
    <h4>Share your money autobiographies</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What is your earliest memory of money?</li>
      <li>How did your family talk about money — or avoid talking about it?</li>
      <li>Did you grow up feeling rich, poor, or somewhere in between? How did that shape you?</li>
      <li>What did your parents teach you about money, directly or by example?</li>
      <li>What money habits from your family do you want to keep? Which do you want to leave behind?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 2 · What Money Means</p>
    <h4>Compare your money symbols</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What does money symbolize for you today — security, freedom, power, love, status?</li>
      <li>When do you feel most anxious about money? Most generous? Most indulgent?</li>
      <li>Are you more of a spender or a saver — and where do you think that came from?</li>
      <li>What's a purchase you've made that felt <em>worth it</em>? What's one you regret?</li>
      <li>If we had $1,000 of truly unplanned money this week, what would each of us want to do with it?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 3 · Work &amp; Meaning</p>
    <h4>Talk about the meaning of your work</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What does your work mean to you — is it a job, a career, or a calling?</li>
      <li>What do you love about what you do? What drains you?</li>
      <li>If money weren't a factor, what would you be doing in five years?</li>
      <li>How does my work affect our relationship — for better or worse?</li>
      <li>What do you need from me to support your work and your dreams?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 4 · Fears &amp; Dreams</p>
    <h4>Share your financial fears and dreams</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What's your biggest fear about money — for us, for our future?</li>
      <li>What would "enough" look like for you?</li>
      <li>What's a dream of yours that money could either enable or block?</li>
      <li>If we had to drastically cut back, what would you <em>not</em> want to give up?</li>
      <li>What does a generous life look like to us — what do we want to give, and to whom?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="exercise-block">
    <p class="ex-label">Exercise 5 · A Shared Philosophy</p>
    <h4>Build the beginning of a shared money plan</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>What are our <strong>top three shared financial goals</strong> for the next 1, 5, and 10 years?</li>
      <li>What's <em>our</em> philosophy on saving, spending, giving, and debt?</li>
      <li>How do we want to divide financial decisions — who handles what, and what do we decide together?</li>
      <li>What's a ritual or rhythm we can build (monthly money check-in, annual review) to keep this conversation going?</li>
      <li>Write one sentence together: <em>"In our relationship, money is for ______."</em></li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <p class="note">The Gottmans emphasize that you don't have to agree on everything — you have
  to <em>understand</em> each other. The numbers come later; the meaning comes first.</p>
