  <h2>What Money Can (and Can't) Buy</h2>
  <p class="theme">Work &amp; Money</p>

  <p>Money issues aren't about dollars and cents — they're about what money <em>means</em>
  to each partner. Money is one of the top five issues that cause conflict in couples, and
  work can become the "third party" in a relationship, demanding time and energy. The goal
  of this date isn't to budget or plan; it's to understand each other's history, values,
  and dreams around work and money.</p>

  <h3>Speed Dating — The Big Ideas</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Stereotyping one of you as the Saver and the other as the Spender isn't helpful — each of you has a family history and instilled values.</li>
    <li>Your work is to <em>understand</em> each other, not to define each other or to force shared values.</li>
    <li>Balancing your relationship and your work is fundamental to the success of your marriage.</li>
    <li>After fidelity and a good sex life, couples report that sharing household tasks is the most important element of a successful marriage.</li>
    <li>When one partner is under enormous stress from work, it creates loneliness and emotional disconnection — it pulls people apart.</li>
    <li>Cultivate gratitude for what you have and the contribution each of you makes.</li>
  </ul>

    <h4>Location</h4>
    <p>This date should cost nothing or as little as possible. If your income has increased
    since you started dating, do something similar to what you did when you had less. If
    you choose a restaurant, pick one where you feel financially comfortable ordering
    anything on the menu.</p>
    <p><strong>Suggestions:</strong> sit in the lobby of a five-star hotel; pack a picnic
    and head to a park with a blanket; or stay home, order from your favorite take-out,
    use the good china, and pamper yourselves with at-home luxury.</p>
    <h4>What to bring</h4>
    <p>Your answers to both questionnaires, so you can compare and discuss. Come prepared
    to share your financial stories and your separate histories and values around work
    and money.</p>
    <h4>Troubleshooting</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>This date isn't about budgets, spending, or numbers — it's about <em>meaning</em>.</li>
      <li>Refrain from judging your partner's values about money. There is no right or wrong way to think about money.</li>
      <li>Never minimize the stress of your partner's work.</li>
      <li>Be honest about what you do and don't do at home — don't measure your work against your partner's.</li>
      <li>Allow yourself to dream about money. Focus on what you have, not past mistakes.</li>
      <li>When your partner shares their dreams, don't disagree or dismiss. Listen, affirm, and ask questions.</li>
    </ul>
