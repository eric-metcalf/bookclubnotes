## About the Book

<div class="about-card" markdown="1">

*Money for Couples* is structured around the idea that most money fights aren't
really about money — they're about identity, security, childhood, and unspoken
expectations. The book gives couples a shared language and a set of concrete
conversations to have *before* the next fight happens.

### What's in it

- **Your rich life, together.** A vision exercise: what do we actually want to spend on, skip, and splurge? Specific, not generic.
- **The four money types.** How each of you relates to money (Avoider, Optimizer, Worrier, Dreamer) and what happens when types collide.
- **Monthly money meetings.** The ritual that replaces fighting: an hour, a calm agenda, the same time each month.
- **Scripts for hard conversations.** Combining finances, prenups, helping parents, spending disagreements — actual language you can borrow.
- **Automation, together.** The money flow set up so neither partner is "the finance person."
- **Kids and money.** What to teach, when, and how.

### How we'll read it

The book is full of dialogue and exercises. Expect to bring your own experience
to the discussion — and to do some of the exercises before or during our
meetings. (All private; sharing is optional.)
